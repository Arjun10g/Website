Profile

I'm Arjunvir Ghumman, a PhD candidate in Quantitative Methods at York University, specialising in machine learning, NLP, and applied statistics. With 7+ years of experience spanning deep learning, statistical modelling, and full-stack research, I build systems that extract insight from complex, messy data.

My work lives at the intersection of transformer-based NLP (BERT, RoBERTa, GPT-Neo), multilevel modelling, and Bayesian inference. I've applied these across mental health research, psychometrics, linguistics, and social science - always with an eye toward reproducibility and real-world impact.

I've delivered 8+ talks and workshops on ML in social sciences, data pipelines, and NLP, and contributed to 9 peer-reviewed publications and manuscripts under review across clinical psychology, psychometrics, and computational methods.

Outside the lab I build web tools with FastAPI + React, teach statistical methods, and consult on research design. I'm looking for ML engineering, data science, or biostatistics roles in industry or research.
